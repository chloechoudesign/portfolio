The Design Solution

Our approach was to build on what worked, not replace it. People loved the humor, the ease, and the classic JibJab look ‚Äî like the big-head style that made our ecards so fun.

MVP Scope & Design Constraints
Keeping It small.
We kicked off the project in early 2024 and aimed to launch right after Q2. That gave us just 3 months to go from design to ship.
Under a tight deadline, we focused our MVP on iOS ‚Äî where most of our paying subscribers were. This gave us the best chance to gain traction quickly and validate the concept early.
We launched the MVP in June 2024, gathered insights from real users, and immediately began refining the experience. Over the next few months, we iterated rapidly before expanding to Web and Android.
Business First Design.
For the early user flow, we created a single, unified flow for both free and paid users ‚Äî selecting a pack, uploading a photo, confirming, and viewing a single-image preview that acted as a soft gate before the experience branched. From there, free users reached the paywall, while subscribers regenerated to see their full results.
This wasn‚Äôt the most elegant UX. Subscribers went through the generating screen twice, which made the experience feel heavier and repetitive.
However, this was a deliberate, business-first decision. A shared flow made the experience faster and simpler to build under tight time and resource constraints. The preview before the paywall created a clear ‚Äúmoment of proof,‚Äù letting users see the AI working and helping build trust and drive conversion.
The flow met our initial business goals, but it also revealed opportunities to improve usability. After launch, we revisited the flow to make the experience lighter and smoother.

Design Iterations
From Generic to Personalized Paywall.
In early explorations, we tested branching the flow right after photo confirmation so paying members could skip extra steps and go straight to their results. While this created a faster experience for subscribers, it sent non members to the paywall too early, before they could fully see their photo integrated into the experience. Without that moment of proof, the upsell felt less convincing.
This led to the personalized paywall. Instead of reusing the generic eCard upsell, we designed a new paywall specifically for Starring You AI that combined the AI generated preview and the paywall into a single step. Users could see their personalized result within the paywall itself, making the upgrade feel like a natural continuation of the experience.
This change also simplified the overall flow. Paying members moved directly from photo confirmation to results, while non members still saw value before deciding to upgrade. The result was a smoother, faster experience that felt more user friendly for everyone.
Adding Skin Tone Selection.
We introduced a skin tone selection step to make results feel more personal and inclusive, then combined it with the photo confirmation screen to keep the flow fast and simple. This helped users feel more accurately represented without adding extra steps.
